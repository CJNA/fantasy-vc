# Turner Novak's tweets as a design reference for Fantasy VC

> Nicole DeTommaso cited Turner Novak (@TurnerNovak, Banana Capital / The Peel) as *the* example
> of building a public VC track record before raising a fund. This doc mines his actual tweets for
> the repeatable **formats** that made VC content engaging + credential-building, and maps each to
> a Fantasy VC design implication.
>
> **Method / honest caveat:** X can't be pulled exhaustively (infinite scroll, thousands of tweets
> since ~2019, aggressive scroll rate-limiting). Sampled ~25 high-engagement tweets via advanced
> search across 2020→2026 (his "olden days" 2020–2022 emphasized, per the ask) plus all-time hits.
> Tweets paraphrased below, not reproduced in full. Sampled 2026-07-22.

## The meta-pattern
Turner's feed **is** the product Nicole describes: a public track record built from short,
number-driven, occasionally-funny posts. Every good one is one of a few repeatable shapes. Fantasy
VC's job is to make each run *auto-generate those shapes* as shareable artifacts. Convergence with
`ICP-PLAYBOOK.md`: her Step 8 (make it public) + his formats = the same feature set.

## The patterns → what they mean for the game

### A · The single-stat gut-punch
One surprising number, framed for disbelief. *"Zoom did a 77% FCF margin. Not a typo."* · *"Quibi
raised $1.8B; all female founders in 2020, $1.9B."* · *"Facebook: ~$190/yr revenue per NA user."*
→ **Card news + reveals should be ONE punchy stat, never a paragraph.** Our daily `NEWS_*`
headlines and the endcard "your best pick did 96×" beat already do this — lean harder: every fog
reveal and round event should read like a Turner stat-drop. *(mostly built; tighten copy)*

### B · The metrics-blind deal tease
*"Anyone want to invest in a SaaS growing 70%/mo — 90% gross margin, bottoms-up GTM, ACV 2×'d this
quarter?"* — describe a deal by 2–3 numbers, hide the name.
→ This is **literally our fog / blind-deal mechanic** (memo.html Diligence Desk). Reference for
phrasing: lead with the two or three metrics that carry the signal, withhold identity. New idea: a
shareable **"name this deal"** tease card from today's board. *(core mechanic validated)*

### C · The public follow-on with a bullet thesis
*"Just doubled down in Jokr's Series A: stacked team · insane value prop · multi-trillion $ market ·
one of the fastest-growing cos I've seen."*
→ Direct validation of **memo + follow-on**. His bullets ARE our memo fields (Team / Market /
Why-now), but *one punchy line each*. New idea: when a player follows on, prompt a one-line **"why I
doubled down"** → a mini public post, Turner-style. *(memo built; add follow-on one-liner)*

### D · The struggle → conviction → vindication arc
The Secureframe story: it was struggling, I knew, I dug in, *"today it's the fastest-growing startup
in the world."* Narrative payoff **over time** is the emotional core.
→ Our endcard already scores the memo against the outcome (*"that call aged well"*). New idea:
auto-generate **"the arc"** for your best pick — foggy early rounds → your conviction → exit
multiple — as a shareable card. This is the single most Turner-shaped artifact we could ship.

### E · The hype-vs-fundamentals tell
*"Everyone at Thanksgiving bought Rivian at IPO. Nobody knows it has zero revenue."*
→ Exactly our **fraud / zero mechanic** (high hype, rotten fundamentals). The emotion is "the crowd
is excited and the numbers say no." Reuse this framing in the **fraud-reveal copy** at season end.
*(mechanic built; borrow the voice)*

### F · Self-aware VC humor / parody math
*"how VCs think startups work."* · **2/22 Ventures**: *"a $22M fund taking 22% at $2.22M ARR growing
22%/mo at $2.22M post."* · the "Inverse [guru] ETF."
→ Tone for our bots + LP letter (already voice-y). The **2/22 parody** is a fun way to teach fund
construction (check → ownership → valuation → growth) — supports the scoped **ownership/valuation
dimension** (P2). New idea: meme-named bot personas beyond Bandwagon Capital.

### G · Participatory / crowdsourced formats
The TikTok "duet-your-startup-pitch," *"message me if you know a startup like this."*
→ Our **challenge links + copy-trading + pods** are this layer. New idea: a **"pitch this board's
mystery deal"** UGC prompt, or a recurring "beat today's board" call-to-action framed as a dare.

### H · Contrarian "left for dead" comeback
*"Snap — that everyone left for dead — just hit its highest valuation ever."*
→ Reinforces our **low-hype-rocket / diamond-in-the-rough** design (unloved deals fill 100%, so the
full check gets in). The best story is backing the name nobody wanted. Keep the daily board's rocket
often low-hype; make the endcard call it out when you caught an unloved winner.

## The build shortlist this implies (new, on top of ICP-PLAYBOOK)
1. **"The arc" share card** (Pattern D) — best pick's struggle→conviction→exit as a Turner-style
   post. Highest-leverage, most on-brand shareable. *small–medium.*
2. **Follow-on one-liner** (Pattern C) — optional "why I doubled down" when following on; joins the
   memo as public-record fodder. *small.*
3. **Turner-tighten the copy** (A/E) — daily news headlines + fraud/round-event reveals rewritten as
   single-stat gut-punches. *small, pure copy.*
4. **"Name this deal" / mystery-deal tease** (B/G) — a blind, metrics-only shareable from the board.
   *medium.*
5. Feeds the scoped **ownership/valuation** dimension (F) and keeps the **low-hype rocket** central (H).
